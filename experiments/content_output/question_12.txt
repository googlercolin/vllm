Question: Find KE of product particles in,
Pi(+) = mu(+) + nu
here Pi(+) is stationary.
Rest mass of Pi(+) &  mu(+) is 139.6 MeV & 105.7 MeV respectively.
A) 2.84 MeV, 26.8 MeV
B) 7.2 MeV, 32.8 MeV
C) 3.52 MeV, 20.8 MeV
D) 4.12 MeV, 29.8 MeV

Assistant Response:
To find the kinetic energy (KE) of the product particles (muon and neutrino) in the given reaction, we can use the principles of conservation of energy and momentum. Given that the pion (Pi+) is stationary, we can use the energy-momentum relation for the particles.

The total energy of the pion before the decay is just its rest mass energy:
\[ E_{\pi^+} = m_{\pi^+} c^2 = 139.6 \text{ MeV} \]

After the decay, the total energy of the muon and neutrino must equal the energy of the pion:
\[ E_{\mu^+} + E_{\nu} = 139.6 \text{ MeV} \]

The total momentum before the decay is zero (since the pion is stationary), so it must also be zero after the decay:
\[ \mathbf{p}_{\mu^+} + \mathbf{p}_{\nu} = 0 \]

Thus, the momenta of the muon and neutrino are equal in magnitude but opposite in direction. We can express their energies in terms of their total energy and their rest mass energies:
\[ E_{\mu^+} = \sqrt{p_{\mu^+}^2 c^2 + m_{\mu^+}^2 c^4} = \sqrt{p_{\mu^+}^2 c^2 + (105.7 \text{ MeV})^2} \]
\[ E_{\nu} = \sqrt{p_{\mu^+}^2 c^2 + m_{\nu}^2 c^4} \approx \sqrt{p_{\mu^+}^2 c^2} = p_{\mu^+} c \]
Here, the neutrino's rest mass is negligible compared to the pion's kinetic energy, so we approximate \( E_{\nu} \approx p_{\mu^+} c \).

Equating the total energy:
\[ \sqrt{p_{\mu^+}^2 c^2 + (105.7 \text{ MeV})^2} + p_{\mu^+} c = 139.6 \text{ MeV} \]

We can solve this equation for \( p_{\mu^+} \):
\[ p_{\mu^+} + \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{105.7 \text{ MeV}}{p_{\mu^+} c}\right)^2} = \frac{139.6 \text{ MeV}}{c} \]

Squaring both sides and simplifying:
\[ p_{\mu^+}^2 + (105.7 \text{ MeV})^2 + 2p_{\mu^+} \sqrt{p_{\mu^+}^2 c^2 + (105.7 \text{ MeV})^2} = (139.6 \text{ MeV})^2 \]
\[ p_{\mu^+}^2 + (105.7 \text{ MeV})^2 + 2p_{\mu^+} \sqrt{p_{\mu^+}^2 + (105.7 \text{ MeV})^2 c^{-2}} = (139.6 \text{ MeV})^2 \]
